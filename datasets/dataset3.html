Arabian Diwan

A journey through Arabic odes and rhymes(55MB)

Dataset Overview

This dataset was carefully collected from ,Al-Diwan, one of the most famous and trusted websites for Arabic poetry. Unlike other websites that may include incorrect, misattributed, or plagiarized poems, Al-Diwan provides reliable content. The collection methodology was not just about gathering random lines and verses; instead, it was organized so that each element in the text column represents a hemistich (half-line). By combining every two consecutive hemistiches, a complete bayt (verse) is formed, which represents the core unit and structure of the poem. This was done to preserve the poetic fabric, rather than simply generating rhymed Modern Standard Arabic text.

Features

  • title: The title of the poem, often the opening line.
  • poet: The poet (author).
  • url: Link to the poem on Al-Diwan.
  • sea: The poetic meter (baḥr) of the poem, which defines its form and rhythm.
  • qafiya: The final rhyme letter at the end of each verse.
  • category: The classification of the poem.
  • text: The poem’s text, where each element is a hemistich, and every two elements together form a verse (bayt).

Data Quality && Data Source

While collecting data from Al-Diwan, my primary focus was on data quality rather than sheer volume. I intentionally excluded rare meters—such as Al-Muqtadab, Al-Madid, and Al-Mudari‘—as their extreme scarcity and limited representation in classical literature would introduce significant noise or class imbalance. For downstream tasks like text generation or classification, these "long-tail" meters often lack enough samples to be statistically useful.

Furthermore, the dataset is fully diacritized (mushakkal), providing a high-fidelity resource for Classical Arabic poetry. While diacritics can be easily stripped for simpler NLP tasks, reconstructing them from scratch is a complex computational challenge. Maintaining them ensures both linguistic authenticity and maximum flexibility for advanced research in phonetics and prosody.
